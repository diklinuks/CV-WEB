Floating Waste Detection

A computer-vision model that detects floating waste in surface water from drone and camera footage — running live, in your browser.

What it does

An object-detection model from the YOLO family identifies floating waste — bottles, plastic fragments, larger debris — in images of surface water. The whole thing runs client-side: the demo above loads the model into your browser and runs inference locally, with no server involved.

How the demo works

The trained model is exported to a web-friendly format and loaded in the browser. You give it an image, it runs a forward pass on your machine, and draws bounding boxes around detected waste with confidence scores. Nothing is uploaded — the inference happens locally.

Technical choices

YOLO over two-stage detectors because inference speed matters for footage, and the accuracy trade-off is small for this task. Training used augmentation for lighting and water-reflection variation, since real footage spans bright midday water, overcast afternoons, and glare — the cases that break models trained only on clean data.
