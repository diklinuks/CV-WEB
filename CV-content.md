### Selected Projects
*Personal and academic builds; each one has a live demo you can run.*

**Betsy: Autonomous procurement agent** · Generative AI · live demo
This one was as much research as code: I did the domain and technical research, then wrote the architecture, decision logs, design and test scenarios before building. Betsy is an AI agent that handles purchasing for a small manufacturer across a 90-day simulation. It watches stock and reorders before items run out when they hit a threshold, scores and ranks suppliers by performance, orders from them, and tracks KPIs. It learns from the orders a human rejects and from sudden events, and pauses for a person to approve the bigger decisions. Everything is explained in the documentation and enforced in the code.

**Eva: Multi-agent marketing team** · Generative AI · *in progress*
You give it something to market (a product, a book, a service) and a team of specialised AI agents work together to produce the whole campaign: audience, strategy, copy and creative concepts, instead of one model doing everything. I'm currently building and testing how the agents split the work and hand off to each other.

**Floating Waste Detection** · Computer vision · live demo
A model that spots floating waste (bottles, cans, cartons, plastic, paper) in water. I built my own dataset of around 8,000 images and tried out seven models, from a simple Random Forest and SVM up to YOLO, before choosing the one that worked best (mAP@50 ≈ 0.87). It runs live in the browser and scores video frame by frame.

**Amsterdam Airbnb: price & market analysis** · Machine learning + data analysis · live demo
I started out trying to predict nightly prices for around 7,800 Amsterdam listings, but the data couldn't support it: the best model only reached about R² 0.23. I used explainability tools to show why the features aren't enough, then switched to clustering the listings into five market segments, shown on an interactive map.
